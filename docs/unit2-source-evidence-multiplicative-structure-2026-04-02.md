## Extracted Evidence

### Direct signed products and quotients

- 미래엔 중단원마무리 page 3 includes an item asking which computation result
  is incorrect among signed multiplication and division expressions.
- This anchors `U2-S5` in direct sign-consistency checks for products and
  quotients, not only in category language or addition/subtraction movement.

### Reciprocal and structured quotient-product reasoning

- 미래엔 중단원마무리 page 3 includes a free-response item that defines the
  reciprocals of two rational numbers and asks the learner to compute a mixed
  quotient-product expression from them.
- 미래엔 중단원마무리 answer/explanation page 5 rewrites each reciprocal
  explicitly before carrying out the quotient and product.
- 발전 문제은행 page 3 also includes a condition problem asking for the value
  of `(x의 역수)÷(y의 역수)`.
- Together these support tutor wording that treats reciprocal handling as part
  of the same multiplicative skill cluster as signed division.

### Even-power and multi-step expression structure

- 발전 문제은행 page 3 includes an item of the form `n이 짝수일 때, 다음 식의
  값을 구하시오.`
- That item requires the learner to keep sign behavior consistent through an
  even-power expression rather than through a single one-step product.
- This is enough to keep `U2-S5` broader than plain multiplication/division
  execution.

### Distributive-law comparison and sign-pattern constraints

- 발전 문제은행 page 5 includes a problem that explicitly says to use
  distributive law (`분배법칙`) to compare two structured expressions.
- The same page also includes sign-determination problems for three numbers
  under product and order conditions.
- These sources justify current `U2-S5` wording around simplifying and
  comparing structured signed expressions, not only computing isolated answers.
