## Extracted Rationale

### `U2-S1 -> U2-S2`

- The source cluster starts with sign and set-language checks before moving into
  midpoint, ordering, and inequality reading.
- A learner who cannot stably separate positive, negative, zero, integer, and
  rational-number language is likely to misread later comparison statements.
- This supports a conservative `HELPFUL` link rather than a `REQUIRED` claim.

### `U2-S2 -> U2-S3`

- The current Unit 2 evidence defines absolute value through distance from the
  origin and through ordering by distance on the number line.
- That makes number-line position reasoning the most direct support for
  absolute-value interpretation.
- This supports a conservative `HELPFUL` link.

### `U2-S1 -> U2-S4`

- The signed-change slice depends on the learner interpreting positive and
  negative values as meaningful directions or changes before calculation.
- Stable sign/category language is therefore a useful support before moving
  into signed addition and subtraction.
- This supports a conservative `HELPFUL` link.

### `U2-S2 -> U2-S4`

- The signed-change slice explicitly uses number-line movement and left-right
  direction to interpret signed addition and subtraction.
- That keeps number-line comparison language as a direct support for the add/
  subtract skill rather than as a separate disconnected topic.
- This supports a conservative `HELPFUL` link.
